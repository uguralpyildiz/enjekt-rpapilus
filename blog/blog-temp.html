CGPs, VCs, Funds, Banks: What are the rules for Financial Website Compliance?
CGPs, VCs, Funds, Banks: What are the rules for Financial Website Compliance?
Documentation
7 min
Published on Dec 13, 2025

Use AI to summarize this article

Key points

In this article:

  • The main regulatory requirements to be aware of when making a banking or financial services website compliant: AMF, MiFID II, PRIIPs, PSD2, DORA, GDPR, ORIAS.
  • A practical checklist to quickly assess and verify your website’s compliance.
Who is this article for?

This article is intended for traditional and private banks, fintech companies, financial investment advisors (CIF / CGPs), asset management companies, investment funds, investment or trading platforms, payment service providers, and more broadly all financial market participants seeking to ensure their website meets regulatory compliance requirements.

Financial professionals are subject to specific regulatory obligations toward their users and clients.
Financial institutions operate in an environment where trust, transparency, and compliance are not optional, but fundamental requirements. Whether addressing retail investors, professional clients, or institutional partners, financial professionals must comply with a strict regulatory framework that governs how information is presented, accessed, and communicated.

As a result, the websites of financial advisors, venture capital firms, investment funds, banks, fintech companies, and other financial services providers must implement additional measures to comply with French and European regulations. A website is no longer just a marketing tool; it is a regulatory interface that may be reviewed by supervisory authorities.

Financial regulations are constantly evolving. New directives, updated guidelines, and reinforced supervisory expectations regularly impact how financial products and services may be promoted online. For this reason, it is strongly recommended to work with a web agency specialized in the financial sector to ensure that your website remains fully compliant over time.

In this article, we outline the key compliance rules that financial institutions must follow in order to reduce regulatory risk and protect both their clients and their brand.
1. MiFID II Directive (European Union)
What is it about?
The MiFID II Directive (Markets in Financial Instruments Directive) is a cornerstone of European financial regulation. It governs how financial market participants present, market, and distribute financial products and services. Its primary objective is to protect investors by ensuring greater transparency, balanced information, and a clear understanding of the relationship between risks and potential returns.

Who is concerned?
MiFID II applies to a wide range of actors, including financial investment advisors (CGPs / CIFs), banks, asset management companies, investment funds, and any entity offering investment services or communicating about financial products, even when the information provided is presented for informational purposes only.

How to be MiFID II compliant on your website?
• Inform users without misleading them or creating unrealistic expectations
• Present risks with the same level of visibility and emphasis as potential benefits
• Provide easy access to regulatory documents such as KIDs, fee schedules, and investment strategies
• Ensure that all financial product information is accurate and kept up to date
2. Directive 2014/65/EU (Legal Basis of MiFID II)
Directive 2014/65/EU forms the legal backbone of MiFID II and establishes detailed rules for investor protection across the European Union. It defines how investors must be categorized and determines which types of information may be made available to them, depending on whether they are classified as professional or non-professional investors.

How to comply?
A specific disclaimer must be implemented on the website to verify the user’s investor status. This mechanism ensures that professional and non-professional investors only access content and products that are appropriate to their profile. In practice, this is often implemented as a pop-up displayed upon entry to the website or when accessing sensitive sections such as fund pages or investment strategy pages.
3. PRIIPs Regulation – Key Information Document (KID)
The PRIIPs Regulation (Packaged Retail and Insurance-based Investment Products) requires the provision of a standardized Key Information Document (KID) for any packaged investment product intended for retail investors. This document is designed to allow investors to easily compare products and understand their essential characteristics.

The KID must include information on risks, performance scenarios, fees and costs, recommended holding periods, liquidity, and other key elements.

On your website, KIDs must be: • Clearly visible, easily accessible, and downloadable
• Provided in their most up-to-date version
• Fully consistent with the information presented on product and fund pages
4. AMF Regulations & Communication Rules
Beyond European regulations, financial institutions targeting a French audience must comply with the communication doctrine of the Autorité des marchés financiers (AMF). These rules govern how financial products and services may be promoted on websites, marketing materials, and digital campaigns.

All content must be clear, accurate, and non-misleading. Risk information must be presented with the same prominence as benefits, and performance data must never be framed as guaranteed or risk-free. Consistency between website content and regulatory documents is essential.

5. PSD2 – Payments & Security
PSD2 (Revised Payment Services Directive) governs online payment services and access to bank account data across the European Union. Its objective is to strengthen payment security while enabling innovation through open banking.

Website compliance under PSD2 is typically achieved either by using a PSD2-compliant payment service provider (PSP) or by becoming a regulated entity subject to full authorization, security, and authentication requirements.
6. DORA – Digital Operational Resilience
DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act) aims to ensure that financial entities can withstand, respond to, and recover from ICT-related incidents such as cyberattacks, system failures, or operational disruptions.

Compliance requires strong IT risk management, robust incident detection and response processes, and regular cybersecurity testing, including vulnerability assessments and penetration tests.
7. IDD – Insurance Distribution Directive
The Insurance Distribution Directive (IDD) regulates how insurance products are presented and distributed across Europe. Its objective is to ensure transparency and consumer protection throughout the distribution process.

Websites must provide clear pre-contractual information and ensure easy access to insurance-specific documents such as the IPID, while maintaining consistency between marketing content and contractual documentation.
8. GDPR & Cookies
What is it about?
Companies operating in the financial sector are not exempt from general data protection obligations. On the contrary, they are fully subject to the GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), which governs how personal data is collected, processed, stored, and used across Europe.

Given the nature of financial activities, websites often handle highly sensitive data, including identity details, financial information, investment preferences, and sometimes banking data. As a result, GDPR compliance is not only a legal requirement, but also a critical trust factor for users and clients.

Who is concerned?
All websites, without exception, must comply with the GDPR. In the financial sector, the stakes are significantly higher due to the volume and sensitivity of the data collected. This applies to banks, fintech companies, asset managers, investment platforms, insurance providers, and financial advisors alike.

How to be compliant?
To comply with GDPR requirements, your website must implement clear and robust data protection measures, including:

• Informing users through a clear and accessible privacy policy explaining how their data is processed
• Collecting only data that is strictly necessary for the stated and legitimate purpose
• Managing user consent, particularly for non-essential cookies and tracking technologies
• Ensuring data security through appropriate technical and organizational measures
• Enabling users to exercise their rights, including access, rectification, erasure, and data portability
Compliance Checklist for Banking & Financial Services Websites
A structured compliance checklist allows financial institutions to quickly assess whether their website meets regulatory expectations across legal, operational, and technical areas. Below is a non-exhaustive overview of the key elements that should be reviewed regularly.

Legal information
• ORIAS registration status, number, and category clearly displayed
• Complete and up-to-date legal notices (legal disclosures)
• Clear company identification details
• Terms & Conditions / Terms of Service easily accessible from all pages

Regulatory documents
• KID (Key Information Document) available and up to date (PRIIPs)
• IPID for insurance products (IDD)
• Prospectuses, reporting documents, and fund documentation available for download
• Full consistency between regulatory documents and website content

Product transparency (MiFID II / AMF)
• Clear, factual content with no promises or misleading claims
• Risks displayed with the same level of prominence as benefits
• Mandatory statement that past performance does not guarantee future results
• Clear identification of promotional content

Investor access & filtering
• Pop-up or disclaimer to distinguish professional and non-professional investors
• Confirmation that users understand access conditions and regulatory constraints
• Restriction of access to sensitive pages when required

Payments & bank account access (PSD2)
• Payments processed via a PSD2-compliant PSP (Stripe, PayPal, Adyen, etc.)
• Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) enabled
• No in-house collection or storage of bank account data
• If acting as a fintech service provider: ACPR authorization and RTS-compliant APIs

Cybersecurity & operational resilience (DORA)
• Documented ICT and IT risk management framework
• Business continuity and disaster recovery plans in place
• Incident detection, monitoring, and response measures
• Regular security testing (penetration tests, vulnerability scans)

Personal data (GDPR)
• Clear and accessible privacy policy
• Compliant Consent Management Platform (CMP), with non-essential cookies only after consent
• Data collection limited to what is strictly necessary
• User rights easily accessible (access, deletion, rectification, portability)

Website content & tools
• Product and service pages kept up to date
• Calculators and simulators that are accurate and non-misleading
• Visible and clear risk warnings on all relevant pages
